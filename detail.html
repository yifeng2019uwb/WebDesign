Ingredients:

Ingredients: Liquid Dye: Water, Maltodextrin, Citric Acid, Xanthan Gum, Sodium Benzoate (Preservative), Potassium Sorbate (Preservative), Sodium Citrate, Benzyl Alcohol, Sodium Hydroxide, Disodium EDTA, Blue 1, Red 3, Yellow 5. Coloring Tablets: Salt, Microcrystalline Cellulose, Sodium Bicarbonate, Citric Acid, Polyethylene Glycol 8000, Magnesium Stearate, Yellow 5, Yellow 6, Blue 1, Red 3.

Directions:

Instructions: Helpful Hints: Read all instructions before coloring eggs. Wash white eggs in cool water with dish washing liquid and sponge. Rinse thoroughly. Note: If you store colored eggs in the refrigerator, the eggs will become wet with condensation. They will smear and temporarily stain your fingers... please use care. Stickers: Place stickers on white or colored eggs. Note: Egg must be completely dry before applying stickers. Glitter Stickers: Peel and stick to dry, white or colored eggs. Speckling: 1. Open one dye packet at a time. Pour each into its own section in the color pallet. 2. Separate the sponges. Insert 1 sponge into the, sponge clip. 3. Dip a sponge into one color. Wipe off the excess dye by blotting on a paper towel. Less color is better! 4. Gently dab the egg with sponge. The less dye used, the better the results will be. Magic Crayon: 1. Write a message or draw a picture on a clean, uncolored egg. 2. Dip egg into color bath and see the message or picture magically appear! Roll-On: 1. Lay the textured roll-on pad onto newspaper. 2. Using a sponge, dab color onto the raised surfaces. 3. Place a colored egg onto the textured roll-on pad and gently roll the egg over the raised surface. Choose several colors or use a single color. Be creative! Banding: 1. Place the bands onto white or colored eggs before placing the egg into a color bath. 2. To make multi-colored eggs, work from light to dark colors. Be careful to use compatible colors. Start with bands on white eggs, color the egg and let dry. Next, band the dried egg and dip into a second color. Let dry. Then, band the dried egg again and dip into a third color. We suggest using 3 colors maximum. Flecking: 1. Place color pallet onto a paper towel. 2. Pour a small amount of each color into the wells of the color pallet. 3. Put a glove on and dip flicking brush into a single color well. (Only a small amount of liquid egg dye is needed.) 4. Hold the flicking brush 6 to 8 inches from newspaper and test your flicking technique (on the newspaper). 5. Once you have a feel for how much dye to use and the amount of flicking required, set a colored egg onto an egg stand. 6. Tape a sheet of newspaper as a back stop. 7. Holding the flicking brush as shown, gently flick the bristles to add the desired amount of natural specs onto your egg - A little at a time! (Less is best.) Flick natural specs onto your eggs simply by rubbing the flicking brush bristles as shown. Note: For a truly natural effect, use the same color liquid egg dye as the color of the egg. For example, pink liquid egg dye on a pink egg. Note: When switching to a new color, wash the brush thoroughly in between colors. Traditional Coloring: Cover the work space with newspaper. 1. Fill 5 cups with 5 oz. of warm water. (Just enough to cover the egg.) Dissolve 1 color tablet in each cup. Note: Using more than 5 oz. of water in a cup will make less colorful eggs. 2. After tablet has dissolved, add 2 tablespoons of white vinegar to each color bath. 3. Bend dipper and lower egg into color bath for 5 minutes. Note: The longer the egg is left in the egg coloring bath, the more colorful the egg will become. 4. Set egg onto egg stands to dry. Egg Stand: Separate egg stands & lock ends together as shown. Use tape if needed.
